Voltage stability analysis using Power System simulation in ETAP

Over frequency surge is observed at generators of base load nuclear power plants in case of line loss contingency due to failure in successful dispersal of generated power. This issue is more probable if multiple units are connected at same bus and alternate transmission lines to load end are not available in case of line loss. Being base load, power plants fail to maneuver electrical output and results in tripping of generators and subsequent tripping of reactors. This situation is highly unfavorable for NPP from operational and economic point of view. A solution to this problem is suggested and simulated on ETAP. Under peak load conditions there are chances of line loss due to line overloading and also results in poor voltage profile at load end. Both problems can be addressed by installation of SVCs (Static Var Compensators) at load end as a source of reactive power. These SVCs not only improve the voltage profile of load end but also reduce reactive power flow and line current to a significant extent. P-V and Q-V curve analysis is used in this work for voltage stability assessment of system. Voltage stability is used as an indicator of steady state stability of the system. Required amount of reactive power compensation is determined by Q-V curve so that the system voltages will remain within pre-defined limit of regulator under normal operation as well as in case of line loss contingency.
